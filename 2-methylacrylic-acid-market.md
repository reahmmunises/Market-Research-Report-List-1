<p><p>The 2-Methylacrylic Acid market is characterized by two primary production methods: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves reacting acetone cyanohydrin with different reagents to yield the acid, often favored for its efficiency and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, the Isobutylene Oxidation Method entails oxidizing isobutylene to produce 2-Methylacrylic Acid, offering a more environmentally friendly process. Both methods cater to a growing demand in various applications, including coatings, adhesives, and plastics.</p></p>
