<p><p>Imaging technology for precision agriculture primarily comprises multispectral and hyperspectral technologies. Multispectral imaging captures data at specific wavelengths across a few bands, facilitating crop health assessments, water stress monitoring, and yield predictions. In contrast, hyperspectral imaging collects detailed information across hundreds of contiguous bands, enabling finer detection of plant stress, nutrient deficiencies, and diseases. Both technologies enhance decision-making by providing valuable insights into crop conditions, helping farmers optimize resource use and improve overall agricultural productivity.</p></p>
