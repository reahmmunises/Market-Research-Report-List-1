<p><ul><li>Limit Fermentation</li><li>Dealcoholization Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low-alcohol beer market consists of two primary types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves controlling yeast activity during brewing to restrict alcohol production, resulting in a lower ABV while maintaining flavor. In contrast, the dealcoholization method removes alcohol from fully fermented beer through techniques like vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis, allowing brewers to preserve the original taste profile. Both methods cater to the growing demand for lower-alcohol beverages, appealing to health-conscious consumers.</p></p>
