<p><p>The non-linear optocouplers market encompasses various types based on their output configurations. Photovoltaic-output optocouplers convert incoming signals into electrical signals using a photodiode, ideal for low-power applications. Transistor-output devices utilize a phototransistor to drive loads, offering high-speed operation. Triac-output models manage AC loads, making them suitable for switching applications. Thyristor-output optocouplers are designed for high-voltage applications. IC-output types integrate functions within a single chip for compact designs. Other variants include specialized configurations for niche applications.</p></p>
