<p><p>Child life insurance encompasses two primary market types: term and permanent child life insurance. Term child life insurance provides coverage for a specified period, typically until the child reaches adulthood, offering affordable premiums but no cash value. In contrast, permanent child life insurance offers lifelong coverage with a savings component, accumulating cash value over time. While term insurance is often selected for its lower costs, permanent insurance offers long-term benefits and financial growth, catering to different parenting and financial strategies.</p></p>
