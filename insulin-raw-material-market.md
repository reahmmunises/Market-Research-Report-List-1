<p><ul><li>Regular Human Insulin</li><li>Insulin Analogue</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The insulin raw material market comprises two main types: Regular Human Insulin and Insulin Analogs. Regular Human Insulin, derived from recombinant DNA technology, closely mimics natural insulin, providing a baseline treatment for diabetes. Insulin Analogs, on the other hand, are modified forms of insulin designed to enhance pharmacokinetic profiles, offering faster or prolonged action. Both types cater to different patient needs, with Regular Human Insulin often being more affordable, while Insulin Analogs provide tailored therapy for better glycemic control.</p></p>
