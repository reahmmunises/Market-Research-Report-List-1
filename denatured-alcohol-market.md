<p><ul><li>Specially Denatured Alcohol (SDA)</li><li>Completely Denatured Alcohol (CDA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The denatured alcohol market comprises primarily two types: Specially Denatured Alcohol (SDA) and Completely Denatured Alcohol (CDA). SDA is treated with specific additives to make it unfit for consumption while retaining some properties for industrial use, catering to sectors like cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. In contrast, CDA undergoes more rigorous denaturing, often with stronger chemicals, rendering it unusable as a beverage. Both types serve various applications, including solvents, fuel, and cleaning products, while ensuring regulatory compliance and preventing misuse.</p></p>
