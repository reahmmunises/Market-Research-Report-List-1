<p><p>The polycarbonate market is primarily categorized into two types: phosgene-based and non-phosgene-based. Phosgene-type polycarbonates are produced using phosgene as an intermediary in the polymerization process, offering advantageous properties such as high clarity and temperature resistance. Conversely, non-phosgene-type polycarbonates are synthesized through alternative methods, which are often more environmentally friendly and safer, reducing reliance on toxic materials. Both types find extensive applications in sectors like automotive, electronics, and construction, appealing to different market needs and regulatory landscapes.</p></p>
